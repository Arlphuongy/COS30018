Methodology

This evaluation compares a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system against a baseline model using multiple metrics:

  • Semantic Similarity: Measures how closely the model's responses match the ground truth answers using cosine similarity of embeddings.
  • Citation Score: Evaluates the model's ability to correctly cite relevant legal documents using F1 score.
  • Hallucination Score: A combined metric where lower scores indicate less hallucination (more factual accuracy).
  • Retrieval Metrics: Includes precision, recall, F1, and MRR to evaluate document retrieval quality.

Hallucination Score Calculation

The hallucination score is a weighted combination of semantic similarity and citation accuracy:

Hallucination Score = 1.0 - (w₁ × Semantic Similarity + w₂ × Citation Score)

where w₁ and w₂ are configurable weights that sum to 1.
